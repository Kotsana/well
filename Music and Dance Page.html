Music and Dance

Now we shall look at the both the dance and music of Basotho as a means of
trying to show how important these aspects are to the nation and its history.

Let us first briefly name and desbribe a few of these dances

  • Mokhibo: A storytelling dance performed by women, using rhythmic shoulder movements to convey legends and values.
  • Litolobonya: Dance Performed by young women during rites of passage, celebrating femininity and strength2.
  • Mohobelo: A vigorous male dance involving stomping and high kicks, often performed during initiation ceremonies.
  • Setapo: A circular group dance symbolizing unity and love, commonly seen at weddings and community gatherings.
  • Herdboysâ€™ Instrumental Dance: Informal dances accompanied by instruments like the lesiba and mamokhorong, played by boys
    while herding livestock.
  • Phathisi: A dance with rattles tied to the legs, producing rhythmic sounds as dancers stomp.
  • Likopelo: Group dances performed with clapping and stamping, often at festivals.
  • Lifela: songs meant for praise performed in gratitude to ancestors.

Now let's dive into genres and sub genres with instruments of Basotho music

  • Lekolulo: A flute traditionally played by herdboys, often used to pass time and communicate across valleys.
  • Liphala: Played with antelope horns or metal horns, often in ceremonies and gatherings.
  • Litolobonya: Women's celebratory music and dance, especially after childbirth.
  • Lits'omo: Storytelling songs, often sung to children with moral lessons.
  • Sesalami: A string instrument similar to the lesiba, producing deep resonant tones.
  • Setinkane: A thumb piano (similar to mbira), producing melodic, metallic tones.
  • Likoma: Initiation school dances for boys, marking transition into manhood.
  • Lithothokiso: Rhythmic recitations, sometimes accompanied by clapping or instruments.
  • Likhapela: Traditional: Traditional group singing, usually in call-and-response style.
  • Herd boys' music:
    1. Lesiba: Mouth-resonated bow instrument
    2. Mamokhorong/Sekhankula: Bowed string instrument
    3. Lekulo: Known as the flute
    4. Setolo-tolo: The jaw harp
